# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers focus on user-centered design, ensuring that product interactions are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs. They bridge the gap between user requirements and technical implementation through research, prototyping, and usability testing.

### Responsibilities
- Lead wireframing and prototyping sessions
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Provide feedback on feature design and user flows
- Create and maintain design systems and UI patterns
- Ensure accessibility and inclusive design practices
- Validate designs through user testing and iteration

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support pain points
- Ensure consistency across the product
- Drive user satisfaction and adoption

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates to align on user requirements and validate design solutions against business goals
- **Developers**: Works closely to implement designs feasibly and provides design specs and assets
- **Project Managers**: Coordinates on design milestones, review cycles, and integration into project timelines

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and recommendations
- Wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Usability test reports and improvement suggestions

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers document processes, product features, API references, and user instructions to ensure clarity and consistency for end users and the team. They standardize language and structure in project artifacts to support onboarding and knowledge sharing.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain internal and public-facing documentation
- Help standardize language and structure in project artifacts
- Support onboarding and training efforts
- Document APIs, features, and processes
- Review acceptance criteria for clarity
- Maintain documentation style guides and templates

### Goals
- Ensure documentation is accurate, clear, and accessible
- Reduce support burden through comprehensive guides
- Enable self-service for users and team members
- Maintain consistency across all documentation

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Product Managers**: Works to capture product vision and feature explanations for user-facing documentation
- **Developers**: Partners to document technical implementation, APIs, and internal processes accurately
- **Project Managers**: Aligns on documentation milestones and ensures artifacts are completed as part of project deliverables

### Typical Communication
- Documentation plans and content reviews
- Style guide updates and templates
- Release notes and user guides
- Internal process documentation and runbooks

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers ensure reliability, scalability, and security of automation pipelines and deployment infrastructure. They build and maintain the systems that enable continuous integration, deployment, and monitoring of applications.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain CI/CD pipelines and cloud infrastructure
- Monitor releases and coordinate incident response
- Implement infrastructure-as-code and observability tooling
- Ensure deployment reliability and system uptime
- Automate operational processes and deployments
- Manage security scanning and compliance in pipelines

### Goals
- Minimize deployment risk and downtime
- Enable fast, automated, and reliable releases
- Maintain high availability and performance
- Provide visibility into system health and metrics

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates on deployment needs, build processes, and troubleshooting production issues
- **Product Managers**: Partners for release planning, incident impact assessment, and feature rollout strategies
- **Project Managers**: Coordinates on deployment schedules, infrastructure dependencies, and incident reporting

### Typical Communication
- Deployment plans and runbooks
- Incident reports and postmortems
- Infrastructure change proposals
- Monitoring alerts and system health dashboards

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business requirements with technical implementation, supporting the decomposition of high-level goals into actionable specifications. They ensure that development work aligns with business objectives and user needs.

### Responsibilities
- Document detailed requirements and business logic
- Facilitate stakeholder interviews and requirement-gathering sessions
- Define acceptance criteria and workflow diagrams
- Analyze business processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Validate development output against requirements
- Support data analysis and reporting needs

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are clear, complete, and testable
- Align technical solutions with business objectives
- Reduce ambiguity and rework through thorough analysis
- Enable data-driven decision making

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Product Managers**: Works closely to translate product vision into detailed requirements and specifications
- **Developers**: Validates development output against requirements and clarifies business logic
- **Project Managers**: Partners on scope definition, requirement prioritization, and stakeholder alignment

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents and user stories
- Process flow diagrams and data models
- Stakeholder interview summaries
- Business impact analyses and feasibility studies

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate Agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team in Agile best practices. They serve the team by protecting them from distractions and helping them continuously improve their processes.

### Responsibilities
- Lead standups, retrospectives, sprint planning, and reviews
- Shield team from scope creep and external interruptions
- Track impediments and facilitate their resolution
- Coach team in Agile principles and practices
- Foster continuous improvement culture
- Facilitate communication and collaboration

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and focus
- Remove blockers quickly and effectively
- Foster self-organization and continuous improvement
- Ensure Agile ceremonies are effective and valuable

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Developers**: Supports by removing impediments, facilitating ceremonies, and protecting team focus
- **Product Managers**: Coordinates on prioritization, backlog refinement, and sprint goals
- **Project Managers**: Collaborates on planning, tracking, and addressing cross-team dependencies

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Impediment tracking and escalation
- Team velocity and burndown metrics
- Retrospective notes and improvement actions

---

## Security Lead

### Role Summary
Security Leads focus on product and infrastructure security throughout the development lifecycle. They ensure that security is integrated into every phase of development, from design to deployment and operations.

### Responsibilities
- Perform threat modeling and security reviews
- Support secure coding practices and compliance requirements
- Coordinate incident response in collaboration with DevOps and PM
- Conduct security assessments and penetration testing
- Review architecture and code for security vulnerabilities
- Maintain security policies and best practices

### Goals
- Minimize security vulnerabilities and attack surface
- Ensure compliance with security standards and regulations
- Enable secure development practices across the team
- Respond quickly and effectively to security incidents

### Interactions with Core Roles
- **Developers**: Advises on security requirements, secure coding practices, and vulnerability remediation
- **Product Managers**: Partners on security feature prioritization and risk-based decision making
- **Project Managers**: Coordinates on security milestones, risk management, and incident escalation procedures

### Typical Communication
- Security review reports and threat models
- Vulnerability assessments and remediation plans
- Security incident reports and postmortems
- Security training and best practice guidance
